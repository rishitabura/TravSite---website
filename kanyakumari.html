About The City

Kanyakumari is the only place on earth, where you can see the Sun Rise and Sun Set from the ocean. It is the only place in India where one can enjoy the unique spectacle of Sunset and Moonrise simultaneously on full moon days. It is the southern tip of Indian subcontinent. The southernmost town in mainland India, it is sometimes referred to as 'The Land's End'.
Nagercoil is the capital of Kanyakumari. Nagercoil is famous for its handicrafts like wood carving, wooden lacquer ware and lace work and embroidery for exports.
Owing to its strategic location, Kanyakumari makes for an ideal tourist destination and a weekend getaway for the ones living in cities like Trichy, Thiruvananthapuram, Pondicherry, Coimbatore and Chennai.


Places to Visit


Vivekananda Rock Memorial




Dedicated to Swami Vivekananda, a religious reformer, the Vivekananda Rock Memorial is one of the most popular tourist attraction in Kanyakumari. Covering an area of six acres, the rock memorial is situated at an island a few metres into the sea and positioned at a level of 17 metres above the sea level. A meditation hall (Dhyana Mandapam) is also attached to the memorial for visitors to meditate. The design of the mandapa incorporates different styles of temple architecture from all over India. It houses a statue of Vivekananda.[18] The rocks are surrounded by the Laccadive Sea.


Thiruvalluvar Statue




The Thiruvalluvar Statue has a height of 29 metres (95 feet) and stands upon a 11.5-metre (38 ft) rock that represents the 38 chapters of "virtue" in the Thirukkural. The statue standing on the rock represents "wealth" and "pleasures", signifying that wealth and love be earned and enjoyed on the foundation of solid virtue.[15] The combined height of the statue and pedestal is 133 ft (40.5 m), denoting the 133 chapters in the Thirukkural. During maintenance work, as well as during rough sea, entry is restricted for tourists.


Gandhi Memorial




The Gandhi Memorial is yet another structure that draws a lot of eyes. The Gandhi Memorial Mandapam has been built on the spot where the urn containing the Mahatma's ashes was kept for public viewing before immersion. Resembling central Indian Hindu temples in form, the memorial was designed in a way that on Gandhi's birthday, 2 October, the first rays of the sun fall on the exact place where his ashes were kept.


Kanyakumari Wax Museum

History buffs can have a good time at Kanyakumari Wax Museum. Set within the premises of Bay Watch (Water Theme Amusement Park), the Kanyakumari Wax Museum is an unmissable attraction for tourists visiting this beautiful city. Built in line with London's Madam Tussauds Wax Museum, this unique attraction displays wax statues of renowned personalities.


Beaches and Waterfalls




Other major travel places at Kanyakumari include a host of beautiful beaches including Sothavilai Beach, Sanguthurai Beach and of course the main Kanyakumari Beach. Kanyakumari Beach is one of the most pristine beaches in the waterfront town of Kanyakumari. It is located at the confluence of the Bay of Bengal, Arabian Sea and Indian Ocean. Beauty oozes here in the form of soft golden sand that is overlapped by shimmering waters of the endless sea. Thirparappu Waterfalls are waterfalls in Kanyakumari District. It is also known as 'Courtallam of Kanyakumari'.


Best time to Visit


The ideal time to visit Kanyakumari is between the months of December to February when you can go sightseeing and participate in water adventures at the beach. Winter Season (December-February) is the best suitable season as during this period, you will find the climate on the pleasant side. Winters are mainly on the pleasant side in Rameshwaram as minimum temperature usually touches nearly 17° C during this season.


About Food


Rice is the staple food of Kanyakumari and is eaten along with chicken curry, pulses, and more. Kothu, Appam, Pazha Sarbath, and Maravazhi Kilangu are some of the most famous food items in Kanyakumari. The Nungu Sarbath is a must try during the summers at Kanyakumari. This cool and refreshing drink is made from palm fruits mixed with Sarbath topped off with some chilling ice. Many varieties of jackfruits are found in Kanyakumari. The colour of some of these fruits is red while some are yellow.


Transportation Facility


By Air

Located at a distance of about 67 kilometres, the nearest airport to Kanyakumari is the Trivandrum International Airport.

By Rail

The Kanyakumari Railway Station (CAPE) is located at a distance of 4 kilometers from the central bus stand. The other nearest railway stations include Nagercoil station (18 kilometers) and Thiruvananthapuram Central (85 kilometers).

By Road

Kanyakumari is connected to the major cities like Trivandrum (86 kilometers), Nagarkoil (19 kilometers), Tirunelveli (91 kilometers), Poovar (66 kilometers) and Kovalam (82 kilometers).
